Summary
Kinkaku-Temple
Officially called Rokuonji Temple, it is one of the chief temples of Shokokuji. It is generally called Kinkakuji Temple because it is especially famous for its "Golden Pavilion".The temple was built in 1397.
Ginkaku-temple
Its official name is Higashiyama Jishoji Temple, and it is one of the sub-temples of Shokokuji. The origin of the name Ginkakuji Temple is said to be the origin of the name Ginkakuji, in contrast to Kinkakuji Temple. It was built in 1482.
Recommended Places
Ryoan-temple
Ryoanji Temple is located about 1 km west of Kinkakuji Temple across Mount Kinugasa. It is one of the "Cultural Properties of Ancient Capital Kyoto" World Heritage Sites. It was built in 1450 by Hosokawa Katsumoto in the Muromachi period (1450). The temple is famous for its vast stone garden with dry landscape garden and Kyoyouchi Pond. The water lilies, which are in full bloom from May to July, are a must-see.
Ginsyadan・Kougetsudai・Kinkyouti
One of the major attractions of Ginkaku Temple is the garden centered around Kinkyouti Pond in front of the Ginkaku and Kannondō, which is flown in front of the temple. The garden, with its distinctive pattern of stepped white sand, is called "Ginsyadan". The Ginsyadan, one of the major attractions of the Ginkaku temple, is said by some to have been built to reflect the light of the moon. It is a unique garden with a pattern that cannot be seen in any other temple in Kyoto.
Speciality
Kinkaku
This candy is sold only at the Kinkaku temple shop. It is decorated with the pattern of the Kinkaku Temple and topped with gold leaf. It is filled with koshi-an (sweetened red bean paste) and has a moderate sweetness to it.
Yatuhashi
Yatsuhashi is one of the most popular Japanese sweets in Kyoto, and as many as 96% of people buy the confectionery as a souvenir of sightseeing in Kyoto. Yatsuhashi was first sold in the 1960s, when the dough was rolled out thin after steaming and cut into a certain size without baking.
