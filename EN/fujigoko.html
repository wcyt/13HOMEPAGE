Motosuko
Motosuko is a lake that straddles the town of Fujikawaguchiko Minami-tsuru-gun and Minobu-cho, Minamikoma-gun, Yamanashi Prefecture. It is located atthe northwestern foot of Mount Fuji, and is one of the five Fuji Five Lakes and its westernmost point. It is the deepest of the Fuji Five Lakes, with amaximum depth of 121.6 m. The lakeshore belongs to Fuji-Kawaguchiko Town (former Kami-Kuikushiki Village, Nishi-Yatsushiro County) and Minobu Town (formerShimomachi Town, Nishi-Yatsushiro County), Minamikoma County, Yamanashi Prefecture, but the boundary of the lake is yet to be determined. It is the deepestof the Fuji Five Lakes and the third largest in area. It is in a special area of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park.
Environment
There are no rivers flowing into or out of the lake, but groundwater is believed to flow back and forth between the lake and the highly permeable scoriah layer of Lake West and Lake Seijin, which are also Fuji Five Lakes. The height of the lake is about 900 meters above sea level, and the water level changes almost in tandem even when it rains. This is because the three lakes were called "Shore Sea" until the early Heian period (794-1192), when Lake Motosu was divided by lava flows from an eruption of Mount Enryaku 2 (800) and the remaining part was divided into Lake West and Lake Shojiko by the great eruption of Jogan (864).

It is located in the south of Yamanashi Prefecture, near the center of Fujikawaguchiko Town. The dammed lake was created by the volcanic activity of Mt. Kawaguchiko
Lake Kawaguchi is located in Yamanashi Prefecture in central Honshu, near Mount Fuji, and is one of the five lakes of the Sagami River system. Fuji Hakone Izu National Park. It has the longest shoreline of the five lakes and is the lowest in elevation. It is the second largest of the Fuji Five Lakes in terms of area and has a maximum depth of 14.6 m, about the same as Lake Shojiko.
Environment
The lake is officially recognized as a target for black bass fishing, and trout are also actively released along with bass, so there are many boat rental shops and fishing tackle shops around the lake. The lake is also known as a mecca for bass fishing and many fishermen visit here every day. However, a large amount of soft lures left at the bottom of the lake has become a problem, and in 2007, the lake became the second lake after Lake Ashi to ban the use of soft lures.
Recommended place
Oisi Park
Oisi Park, in which Kawaguchiko Shizen Seikatsukan is located, is located in the Oishi area on the north shore of Lake Kawaguchi. It is known as a lavender park along with Yagisaki Park on the south shore, and is the main venue for the "Kawaguchiko Half Festival" where lavenders begin to bloom at the end of June and continue to do so until mid-July.

Yamanakako
Lake Yamanakako is a freshwater lake located in Yamanakako Village, Minami Tsuru County, Yamanashi Prefecture. It is one of the Fuji Five Lakes. With an area of 6.57 km2, it is the largest of the Fuji Five Lakes. It is the highest among the Fuji Five Lakes and the third highest in Japan. The depth of the lake is 13.3 meters, the deepest among the Fuji Five Lakes, and it has been designated as a Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park. Fuji Hakone Izu National Park, also known as "Gagyu Lake" because of its cow-like shape. Fuji: An Object of Faith and a Source of Art" and has been inscribed as a World Cultural Heritage Site.
Environment
Due to the high elevation and shallow depth of the lake, the entire surface of the lake sometimes freezes during the cold season, and in recent years, almost the entire surface of the lake was found to be frozen on January 10, 2006. The Hirano Bay area, which is separated by a sand spit called "Misaki" (commonly known as Cape Omama), is shallow and inhabited by carp, Japanese ducks and smelt.
