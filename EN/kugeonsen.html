Summary
Kusatu Onsen
It is famous as one of Japan's most famous hot springs, and is a lively hot spring resort with many different types of inns and souvenir shops centered around the famous "hot water field". Strongly sterilizing hot springs gush forth from many places in the town, and you can tour the hot springs for free.
Gero Onsen
Gero Onsen is a hot spring located in Gero City, Gifu Prefecture (formerly known as Hida Province, Gero Town, Mashida County, Gifu Prefecture). It is known as one of Japan's three best hot springs, along with Arima and Kusatsu Onsen, and is therefore known as one of Japan's three best springs.

Gero Onsen, the tipping point effect

The technical term for this is the "Tonochi Effect," which refers to the stimulating effect of going to an onsen resort and getting a boost in blood circulation from the unique chemical ingredients of the hot springs, eating the local seasonal food, and walking through the scenery and streets that you would not normally see.
This is one of the effects that can be felt in any hot spring resort, but here in Gero Onsen, there are many things to see and do when strolling around town, so you can expect a greater tipping point effect.
If you're looking for a break from years of accumulated stress, this is also a great place to visit.

Hotel Recommendations
Kusatu Onsen Hotel Sakurai
「Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Sakurai is located in the central plateau of Kusatsu Onsen, a hotel with a rich Japanese style. A variety of rooms are available, including calm Japanese-style rooms, rooms with an open-air bath, and sukiya-zukuri-zukuri Japanese-style rooms, so you can choose the one that best suits your scene, number of guests, and budget. The 30-meter-long bath, one of the largest in Kusatsu, is a relaxing space with plenty of openness. You can enjoy three fountainhead sources, "Bandai-no-koto, Nishinokawara-mixed" and "Watanoyu", without adding water, and this is the perfect hot spring bath for enjoying the hot spring. In the inn, the Yumomi Show and the Sakuradaiko Drum Show are held every day from 8:30 p.m. to the accompaniment of Kusatsu's folk music, and are sure to be enjoyed by children and adults alike!
Gero Onsen Hotel Kasakabe Almeria
Gero Onsen Hotel Kusakabe Almeria was founded in 1932 and is a long-established ryokan with four buildings along the Hida River. You can enjoy the smooth, soft-textured hot water of Gero in three different large baths - an open-air bath, a view bath and a cypress bath - as well as in two private baths. The inn is equipped with four restaurants that offer meals in rooms that make the most of seasonal ingredients, as well as Japanese, Chinese and Western cuisine. There are a variety of guest rooms to suit all kinds of relaxation styles, including a Japanese-Western-style room equipped with both tatami mats and a carpeted room with twin beds. A tour of the hotel's interior, which takes guests around the works of famous artists such as Yokoyama Taikan, is also popular.
Festival

Summer Festival in Kusatsu Onsen

In Kusatsu Onsen, two big festivals are held every summer and the whole town is very busy.

■ Shirane Shrine Festival

Shirane Shrine, dedicated to Nippon Muso, is located about a minute's walk from Yubata. During the annual summer Ujigami Festival, a parade of portable shrines carried by the townspeople takes place in the town, and stalls are set up around Yubatake. It is a fun summer festival for locals and tourists alike.

■ The 74th Kusatsu Onsen Thanksgiving Festival

The Kusatsu Onsen Thanksgiving Festival is held on August 1st and 2nd every year to pray for good health and to thank the hot springs.

The rituals such as "the ritual of drawing the source of spring water", "the ritual of the diversion of spring water", and "the dedication of hot water" by the goddess chosen from the townspeople are performed solemnly. It is a festival unique to the hot spring resort, and many guests say that they are glad to come to the festival.

Gero Onsen Festival

This is the largest summer event in Gero Onsen, with the Dragon God Fire Festival at the top of the agenda, a mikoshi parade and Gero dancing, and a fireworks musical on the third day. On the first day of the Dragon God Fire Festival, five dragons and the portable shrine parade through the town, culminating in a gathering at Shirasagi Bridge in the center of the hot spring town. Amid sparks of fire, the sound of roaring firecrackers and the fantastic flames of torches, the dynamic men perform a heroic and energetic dance. On the second day, traditional Gero dancing and a parade of samba teams will take place, and on the third day, computer-generated special fireworks displays with sound and light will light up the night sky in the hot spring town. The fourth day of the festival is the Summer Festival in Gero Onsen, a music festival.
