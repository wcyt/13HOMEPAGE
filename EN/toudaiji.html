Summary
Toudaiji
Todaiji is a temple of the Kegon sect of Buddhism, located in Zoshi-cho, Nara City, Nara Prefecture. It is also called Konkomyo Shitenou Gokoku-no-Tera, and was built by Emperor Shomu in the Nara period (8th century) with all the strength of the nation. The temple is dedicated to Rushana Buddha, known as the "Great Buddha of Nara".
Recommended Place
Toudaiji Nandaimon
The temple was restored in 1199 during the Kamakura period (119) after it was destroyed by a typhoon in August of the Heian period (962). It is famous for its use of the Daibutsu-sama, also known as Tenchu-sama, which is said to be an architectural style introduced from the Song dynasty in China by the founder of Todaiji's revival, Shunyobo Jugen. On the upper level, there is a plaque in the center of the front of the building that reads "Da Kegonji". This plaque was renewed to coincide with the Buddhist memorial service on October 10, 2006, to commemorate the 800th anniversary of the death of Shugen Shonin, based on an old record of such a plaque.
Festival
Setsubun and Star Festival
During the day, Gengu and Setsubun mame-maki are held. Gengu is a ceremony to raise old money and good luck charms and other items to the fire. The bean-throwing ceremony for Setsubun is held at around 2:00 p.m. on the stage of Nigatsudou. The "Hoshi Matsuri" is a puja to pray to the stars for good luck and peace. In the evening, the puja is held at the main hall of the Nigatsudou hall with a ten thousand torches and a star mandala.
