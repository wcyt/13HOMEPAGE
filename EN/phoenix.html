Summary
Byoudouinn
During the day, Gengu and Setsubun mame-maki are held. Gengu is a ceremony to raise old money and good luck charms and other items to the fire. The bean-throwing ceremony for Setsubun is held at around 2:00 p.m. on the stage of Nigatsudou. The "Hoshi Matsuri" is a puja to pray to the stars for good luck and peace. In the evening, the puja is held at the main hall of the Nigatsudou hall with a ten thousand torches and a star mandala.
Recommended Place
Kannnonndou
It was built in the early Kamakura period on the site of Byodoin, where the main hall used to be when Byodoin was built. In the beginning, there was only a shumidan in the center of the hall, and it seems to have served the role of a dojo, so the interior space is simple. It seems to have played a role as a dojo, and the interior space is simple and simple, but the rafters are double-layered, in keeping with the prestigious style of the Tenpyo era (mid-8th century). It was designated as a former national treasure in 1902 and again designated as a national important cultural property after the war.
